SARS-CoV-2 Variants Genomic Analysis

A comparative study on nucleotide composition and structural variations of international variants.

Iker Ambriz Rodríguez Tecnólogico de Monterrey
Fundamentals

What is SARS-CoV-2?

It is the coronavirus responsible for the COVID-19 pandemic. It has a positive-sense single-stranded RNA genome (+ssRNA), with a length of approximately 29,900 nucleotides.

Variants arise due to random errors (mutations) during viral replication, generating genomes with unique sets of changes.

Alpha United Kingdom
Delta India
Omicron South Africa
Methodology

Computational Analysis

Analyzed Data

FASTA files processed with R. Sequences from:

  • • Bolivia (S.C. De La Sierra)
  • • USA (Massachusetts)
  • • Japan

Executed Tests

Use of the seqinr library to determine:

  • • Base count (A, C, G, T)
  • • GC content percentage
  • • Total length and complements

Significance

Knowing the genomic sequence allows tracking the spread, identifying new mutations, and updating vaccines or PCR tests to maintain their efficacy.

Key Findings

Relationship Between Sequences

1

Insertions and Deletions

Notable variations in total length were detected. If these sequences represent the same gene, this indicates major structural changes that could alter the 3D shape of viral proteins.

2

Substitution Mutations

Variations in the GC percentage confirm nucleotide substitutions. A single letter change can alter an amino acid, driving the virus's evolution and adaptation.

Study Limitations

This analysis is purely descriptive and statistical. The biological implications mentioned (such as antibody evasion) are theoretical inferences based on current scientific literature and not direct findings from this evidence's data analysis. Protein modeling and functional assays would be required to confirm these hypotheses.
